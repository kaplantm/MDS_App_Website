A Inclusive User-Focused Approach

By developing an accessible biological diagram interface, the Multimodal Diagram System aims to increase access to S.T.E.M. diagrams, providing B.V.I. (Blind and Visually Impaired) students the educational tools they need to pursue the sciences in education and the workforce. These students generally require alternate educational materials to access the same information and resources available to non-visually impaired students. Adapted materials for students with significant vision loss (non-functional vision) are often limited in availability, expensive, bulky and uni-modal. Even when adapted materials are provided, vision loss presents a considerable obstacle in learning information typically represented in visual diagrams. The prevalent use of diagrams in S.T.E.M. fields makes these already challenging fields especially difficult for B.V.I. students to access and understand. Therefore, the premise of this research and the resulting Multimodal Diagram System is to provide a free tool for use in creating and presenting scientific educational diagrams using existing multi-purpose commercial hardware with minimal expertise.

The uses of the Multimodal Diagram System extends beyond the B.V.I. demographic. This system could be used in a general or mixed class settings to provide multi-sensory interactive diagrams to sighted students. This would be especially beneficial in mixed-ability classrooms, in which B.V.I. student’s hardcopy materials may not have a one-to-one correspondence to the references used by sighted students. Teachers using this interface would be able to produce customized diagram displays for B.V.I. students, providing them the tools they need to be actively involved in classroom discussion.
